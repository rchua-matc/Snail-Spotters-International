This is the Iowa Pleistocene Snail.

An Endangered Relic of the Ice Age

The Iowa Pleistocene snail (Discus macclintocki) inhabits the Driftless Area of the Midwestern United States. Fossil records can trace the gastropod back almost 400,000 years to the Pleistocene Epoch: the last time that the world was buried under ice. Due to climate change and environmental destruction by humans, the Iowa Pleistocene snail is a federally-listed endangered animal. Who We Are

Snail Spotters International is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization focused on conservation and education related to the Iowa Pleistocene snail. We have 15 chapters in 8 countries, including the Australia, Canada, Germany, Japan, Madagascar, Mexico, Singapore, and the United States. Snail Spotters around the globe hold monthly public education meetings as well as bi-weekly excursion trips to track local populations. Interested in joining? Click here to find the nearest chapter.
